Feeding Guidelines

1 When should my child first see a dentist?

Your child should visit a pediatric dentist when the first tooth comes in, usually between 6 and 12 months of age. This visit will establish a dental home for your child. Early examination and preventive care will protect your child's smile now and in the future.

Why so early?

Dental problems can begin early. Early Childhood Caries (baby bottle tooth decay) can start as soon as teeth appear. The earlier the dental visit, the better the chance of preventing problems. Children with healthy teeth chew food easily and smile with confidence.

2 Preventing Bottle and Nursing Tooth Decay

✓ Do's
  • Wean from bottle at 12-14 months
  • Offer juice only in a cup with meals
  • Use only water in bedtime bottles
✗ Don'ts
  • Don't allow sleeping with bottles containing milk/juice
  • Avoid at-will breast-feeding after first teeth erupt
  • Don't give juice from bottles

3 Thumb and Finger Sucking

Normal for babies: Thumb sucking is perfectly normal for infants; many stop by age 2.

When to be concerned: If the habit continues beyond age 3, a professional evaluation is recommended as it can create crooked teeth or bite problems.

4 Cleaning Your Baby's Teeth

👶 Birth to First Tooth

Clean gums with a soft infant toothbrush or cloth and water

🦷 Under 2 Years

Use a "smear" of fluoridated toothpaste with a soft, age-appropriate toothbrush twice daily

👧👦 Ages 2-5

Use a "pea-size" amount of toothpaste. Parents should perform or assist with brushing

5 Teething Advice

From six months to age 3, your child may have tender gums when teeth erupt.

Soothing Options:
  • Clean teething ring
  • Cool spoon
  • Cold wet washcloth
  • Chilled teething ring
  • Gentle gum massage with clean finger

💧 Fluoride Treatment

Over 50% reduction in cavities

Community water fluoridation has dramatically lowered decay rates

Fluoride is a natural compound that strengthens tooth enamel and prevents cavities. It works by inhibiting mineral loss from teeth and encouraging remineralization of weakened areas. Fluoride also affects cavity-causing bacteria, reducing acid attacks that break down teeth.

Hall Technique

No-Drill Crown Placement

Stainless steel crowns without anesthesia or tooth preparation

Fillings & Restorative Care

🌟 Restoring Function, Health & Beautiful Smiles

Advanced techniques to repair and strengthen your child's teeth

💚 What is Restorative Dentistry?

Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing and restoring teeth to their best health. This includes removing decay, repairing damage, and replacing defective teeth. The goal is to improve tooth function, oral health, and maintain your child's beautiful smile.

🤔 Why Do Kids Need Restorative Dentistry?

Baby teeth may fall out, but they still need protection! Here's why restorative care is important:

• Baby teeth guide permanent teeth into proper position
• Essential for proper chewing and digestion
• Critical for speech development
• Prevents spread of decay to surrounding teeth
⚠️ When Should You Schedule an Appointment?
Sudden tooth sensitivity Tooth pain Cracked or chipped tooth Visible cavities Dental trauma

Space Maintainers

Baby teeth hold space for permanent teeth

Some baby teeth aren't replaced until age 12-14!

Space maintainers are custom-fitted metal appliances that hold space when a baby tooth is lost too early. They're small, unobtrusive, and most children adjust to them easily within a few days.

⚠️ What Happens When Baby Teeth Are Lost Too Early

❌ Without Space Maintainer
  • Adjacent teeth tilt into empty space

  • Permanent teeth become crowded
  • May require extensive orthodontics
✅ With Space Maintainer
  • Remaining teeth stay in position
  • Space preserved for permanent tooth

  • More affordable than future treatment

Sedation Dentistry

Most dental procedures in children are completed using local anesthesia, however, very young, fearful children may need added help to control anxiety. Our office is licensed to use the safest clinical approaches to provide mild sedation or oral sedation. Issaquah Pediatric Dentistry follows the administration guidelines for safe sedation endorsed by the American Academy of Pediatrics and American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry. Using sedation safely is extremely important and this is why Dr. Bansal dedicates many continuing education hours specific to this topic.

😌 Nitrous Oxide (Laughing Gas)

Safe, effective sedation for anxious children

The safest sedative in pediatric dentistry - fully reversible with no lingering effects

Comfortable nasal mask delivery

Most children are naturally comfortable in our pediatric dental office. However, some children may feel anxious or need extra support. Nitrous oxide/oxygen is a safe, effective sedative that calms fears, enhances communication, and works well for children with sensitive gag reflexes.

🧪 What is Nitrous Oxide/Oxygen?

N₂O-O₂: A safe blend of nitrous oxide and oxygen delivered through a comfortable nasal mask. Your child breathes normally, uptake occurs through the lungs, and it's completely eliminated after breathing oxygen for a few minutes - no lingering effects.

  • Possible "tingly" feeling in arms/legs
  • Some children report dreaming
✨ Benefits

  • Raises pain threshold
  • Makes time feel shorter
  • Often called "laughing gas"

💤 General Anesthesia in Clinic

Complete unconsciousness for complex treatments

Administered by specially trained professionals in equipped facilities

🏥 What is General Anesthesia?

Controlled unconsciousness that eliminates awareness, movement, and discomfort during dental treatment. Administered by physicians or dentists with specialized training using various medications for complete patient comfort.

👶 Who Should Receive General Anesthesia?
🚫 Behavioral Challenges
  • Extremely uncooperative children
  • Severe fear or anxiety
  • Very young children who can't cooperate
🦷 Treatment Complexity
  • Extensive dental needs
  • Significant surgical procedures
  • Multiple treatments in one visit
🏥 Special Needs
  • Special health care needs
  • Medical conditions
  • Developmental considerations
🛡️ Is General Anesthesia Safe?

Yes, when properly administered. While there are inherent risks, general anesthesia can be used safely and effectively when administered by appropriately-trained professionals in properly-equipped facilities.

🍼 Why Do Children Suck on Fingers, Pacifiers or Objects?

Completely normal behavior! Sucking provides security for babies and young children. It's a natural way for young babies to make contact with and learn about the world. Babies even begin sucking their fingers or thumbs before birth.

🦷 Are These Habits Bad for Teeth and Jaws?
✅ Normal Timeline

Ages 2-4: Most children naturally stop on their own

⚠️ Prolonged Habits
  • Upper front teeth tip toward lip
  • Teeth don't come in properly
  • Affects bite alignment
  • Impacts jaw and bone growth
⏰ When Should I Worry About a Sucking Habit?

Dr. Bansal carefully monitors your child's tooth eruption and jaw development, keeping sucking habits in mind. Intervention may be recommended for children beyond 3 years of age because persistent habits can cause long-term problems.

🍼 Pacifiers vs. Thumbs/Fingers
📊 Effects

Thumb, finger, and pacifier sucking affect teeth and jaws in essentially the same way

✅ Advantage

Pacifier habits are often easier to break than thumb/finger habits

Emergency Dental Care

⚡ When your child needs urgent dental treatment, your pediatric dentist stands ready to help

Please keep our emergency number available and convenient. We have same day appointments available!

🦷

Baby Tooth Knocked Out

Contact your pediatric dentist as soon as possible. The baby tooth should not be replanted because of the potential for subsequent damage to the developing permanent tooth.

🚨

Permanent Tooth Knocked Out

Unlike a baby tooth, an adult tooth should be put back into the socket.

Quick Action Steps:

  1. Find and rinse tooth gently in cool water (no soap!)
  2. Replace in socket immediately with clean gauze
  3. If unable: hold under tongue or in cheek
  4. Place in cold milk or saliva if needed
  5. Get to dental office immediately

⏰ The faster you act, the better your chances of saving the tooth!

😣

Toothache

Call your pediatric dentist and visit the office promptly.

Comfort Measures:

  • Rinse mouth with water
  • Children's pain medication (proper dosage)
  • Cold compress on face (not heat!)
  • ❌ Do NOT use heat or aspirin

Chipped or Fractured Tooth

Contact your pediatric dentist immediately. Quick action can save the tooth, prevent infection and reduce the need for extensive dental treatment.

Immediate Care:

  • Rinse mouth with water
  • Apply cold compresses if lip injured
  • Save tooth fragment in cold milk/water
  • Bring fragment to dental office
🚑

Severe Head/Jaw Injury

⚠️ You need immediate medical attention

A severe head injury can be life threatening. Emergency medical teams may reach you faster than you can get to the hospital.

🦷 Treatment Questions

Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth to prevent cavities. They're typically recommended when the permanent molars come in (around ages 6 and 12). Sealants can reduce cavity risk by up to 80% and are usually covered by dental insurance.

Nitrous oxide is a safe, mild sedation option for anxious children or those undergoing longer procedures. It helps children relax while remaining conscious and responsive. It's completely reversible and wears off quickly after treatment. We assess each child individually to determine if nitrous oxide would be beneficial.

SDF is a non-invasive treatment that stops cavity progression and prevents new cavities. It's applied as a liquid and is particularly useful for young children, those with special needs, or when traditional drilling isn't possible. The treated area may turn black, but it's an effective way to arrest decay and buy time for more comprehensive treatment.

🛡️ Prevention & Habits

Never put your baby to bed with a bottle containing milk, formula, or juice. Clean your baby's gums with a soft cloth after feeding. Start brushing with fluoride toothpaste as soon as teeth appear. Wean from bottle by age 12-14 months and transition to a sippy cup with water between meals.

Most children naturally stop these habits between ages 2-4. If the habit continues past age 4-5 or when permanent teeth begin erupting, it may affect tooth alignment and jaw development. We can provide guidance and, if necessary, habit-preventing appliances to help break persistent habits.

Custom mouth guards are recommended for contact sports and activities with risk of facial injury. We create properly fitted guards that provide superior protection compared to store-bought options. They're especially important for children with braces or other orthodontic appliances.
